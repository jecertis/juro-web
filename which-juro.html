Juro Compliance Scanner — not the contract tool

Juro · 2 June 2026 · ~1 min read

If you landed here looking for contract lifecycle management software, you want juro.com — a separate company that helps legal and ops teams manage contracts. JeCertis (trading as Juro at jurocompliant.com) builds compliance scanning software. The two products solve different problems and have no relationship.

What does JeCertis actually do?

  • Scans your public-facing surface and codebase for regulatory obligation markers. The scanner reads privacy policies, consent implementations, and data handling disclosures against GDPR, India's DPDP Act 2023, and DORA requirements. It reports what it detects versus what regulations require. Absence of a finding is not a compliance verdict; it is a gap signal.
  • Produces signed, deterministic artefacts. Every scan generates a cryptographically signed output tied to a target, a ruleset hash, and a timestamp. The artefact is customer-held — no infrastructure state leaves your perimeter. Any auditor or counterparty can independently re-verify the finding without trusting JeCertis.
  • Runs non-custodially, inside your perimeter for deep scans. The Tier 3 agent deploys inside your VPC (private cloud network) under a read-only IAM (access permissions) role. It maps data flows, encryption state, and audit log coverage locally. The only outbound call is a SHA-256 fingerprint to a public notary log — no PII or infrastructure metadata leaves your environment.

Who this is for

DPOs, CTOs, and compliance leads at India-facing SaaS companies managing DPDP obligations — and at companies with EU operations managing GDPR Article 28 processor obligations or DORA ICT risk requirements. Particularly useful when a signed DPA or processor agreement exists but no technical evidence backs the obligations stated in it.

Frequently asked questions

Is JeCertis the same company as juro.com?

No. JeCertis (trading as Juro at jurocompliant.com) is a compliance scanning tool. juro.com is a separate company that makes contract lifecycle management software. The similar names cause confusion — that is why this page exists.

What regulations does the Juro compliance scanner cover?

The scanner covers GDPR (EU and UK), India's DPDP Act 2023, and DORA (EU Digital Operational Resilience Act). Each scan produces a posture score and a signed, deterministic JSON artefact. No data leaves your infrastructure.

What does a signed artefact prove?

A signed artefact is a cryptographically hashed snapshot of your compliance posture at a specific point in time. It proves what your public surface looked like when the scan ran — useful as evidence for auditors, DPAs (Data Protection Authorities), and counterparties. It does not assert that you are compliant; it records what was found.
